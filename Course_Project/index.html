Restaurant Management System

Description

This system is designed to manage various operations in a restaurant, including:

  • Customer queue management for efficient service.
  • Table reservation system to handle bookings and availability.
  • Inventory management to track items and stock levels.
  • Menu and billing to manage food orders and total revenue.
  • Delivery system for calculating shortest routes for delivery.
  • Lodging system for managing room bookings and availability.

The goal of this system is to enhance operational efficiency, providing an organized method to manage customers, reservations, inventory, and other restaurant-related services.

Usage of Algorithms

The system leverages several key algorithms to optimize various operations:

  • Queue Management Algorithm: A simple queue algorithm ensures customers are served in the order they arrive. Customers are added and removed using First-In-First-Out (FIFO) principle.
  • Shortest Path Algorithm: Dijkstra's algorithm is used in the delivery system to find the shortest route between two points, improving delivery time and efficiency.
  • Reservation Management Algorithm: A lookup mechanism allows for efficient reservation and release of tables by keeping track of the tables and their current status.
  • Inventory Update Algorithm: Inventory updates are managed with simple addition and subtraction operations based on stock quantities to ensure availability tracking is up-to-date.
  • Billing and Revenue Calculation: A basic accumulation algorithm adds up the cost of items ordered by customers and splits the total bill among the specified number of people.

These algorithms work together to streamline the workflow and enhance the restaurant's overall operational efficiency.
